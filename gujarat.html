Location Covered:

Dwarka

Dwarka is an ancient city in Gujarat, India, considered one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism. It is believed to be the kingdom of Lord Krishna, a revered Hindu deity, and is mentioned in the epic Mahabharata.
The city is situated on the banks of the Gomti River and is home to various ancient temples, including the famous Dwarkadhish Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna.

Gir

Gir has been used by the Gujarat State Forest Department which formed the Indian Crocodile Conservation Project in 1977 and released close to 1,000 marsh crocodiles into Lake Kamaleshwar and other small bodies of water in and around Gir

Kirti Mandir


Kirti Mandir is a shrine in Porbandar, Gujarat, India, dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi and his wife, Kasturba
The museum is located in the ancestral home of the Gandhi family, where Mahatma Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869
The memorial was established in 1950 and contains life-size oil paintings of Mahatma and Kasturba Gandhi

Statue of Unity

The design showing Patel's dhoti-clad legs and sandled feet make the statue narrower at the base with a slenderness ratio that varies between 16 and 19, significantly higher than most tall buildings with ratios between 8 and 14

Sun Temple

Location: Modhera village, Mehsana district, Gujarat, India
Special feature: The sanctum sanctorum is designed to catch the first rays of the rising sun during the solar equinoxes
